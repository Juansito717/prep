Exercise 9.

We now understand the effects of the transformation \(h(t) = f(kt)\) where \(k \gt 0\) for a given function \(f\text{.}\) Our goal is to understand what happens when \(k \lt 0\text{.}\)
  1. We first consider the special case where \(k = -1\text{.}\) Let \(f(t) = 2t - 1\text{,}\) and let \(g(t) = f(-1 \cdot t) = f(-t) = -2t - 1\text{.}\) Plot \(f\) and \(g\) on the same coordinate axes. How are their graphs related to one another?
  2. Given any function \(p\text{,}\) how do you expect the graph of \(y = q(t) = p(-t)\) to be related to the graph of \(p\text{?}\)
  3. How is the graph of \(y = \sin(-3t)\) related to the graph of \(y = \sin(t)\text{?}\)
  4. How is the graph of \(y = \cos(-3t)\) related to the graph of \(y = \cos(t)\text{?}\)
  5. Given any function \(p\) and a constant \(k \lt 0\text{,}\) how do you expect the graph of \(y = q(t) = p(kt)\) to be related to the graph of \(p\text{?}\)
  6. How are \(\sin(-t)\) and \(\sin(t)\) related? How are \(\cos(t)\) and \(\cos(-t)\) related?
