Example 4.1.6.

Consider a right triangle in which one of the non-right angles is \(\beta = \frac{\pi}{5}\) and the leg opposite \(\beta\) has length \(4\text{.}\)
Determine (both exactly and approximately) the measures of all of the remaining sides and angles in the triangle.
Solution.
From the fact that \(\beta = \frac{\pi}{5}\text{,}\) it follows that \(\alpha = \frac{\pi}{2} - \frac{\pi}{5} = \frac{3\pi}{10}\text{.}\) In addition, we know that
\begin{equation} \sin\left(\frac{\pi}{5}\right) = \frac{4}{h}\tag{4.1.5} \end{equation}
and
\begin{equation} \cos\left(\frac{\pi}{5}\right) = \frac{x}{h}\tag{4.1.6} \end{equation}
Solving Equation (4.1.5) for \(h\text{,}\) we see that
\begin{equation} h = \frac{4}{\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{5}\right)}\text{,}\tag{4.1.7} \end{equation}
which is the exact numerical value of \(h\text{.}\) Substituting this result in Equation (4.1.6), we find that
\begin{equation} \cos\left(\frac{\pi}{5}\right) = \frac{x}{\frac{4}{\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{5}\right)}}\text{.}\tag{4.1.8} \end{equation}
Solving this equation for the single unknown \(x\) shows that
\begin{equation*} x = \frac{4 \cos\left(\frac{\pi}{5}\right)}{\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{5}\right)}\text{.} \end{equation*}
The approximate values of \(x\) and \(h\) are \(x \approx 5.506\) and \(h \approx 6.805\text{.}\)
