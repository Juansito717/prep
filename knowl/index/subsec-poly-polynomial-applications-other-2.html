A different use of polynomial functions arises with Bezier curves. The most common type of Bezier curve used in applications is the cubic Bezier curve, which is a curve given parametrically by a formula of the form \((x(t), y(t))\text{,}\) where
\begin{equation*} x(t) = (1-t)^3 x_0 + 3(1-t)^2 t x_1 + 3(1-t) t^2 x_2 + t^3 x_3 \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} y(t) = (1-t)^3 y_0 + 3(1-t)^2 t y_1 + 3(1-t) t^2 y_2 + t^3 y_3\text{.} \end{equation*}
The curve passes through the points \(A = (x_0,y_0)\) and \(B = (x_3, y_3)\) and the points \(C = (x_1, y_1)\) and \(D = (x_2, y_2)\) are called control points. At http://gvsu.edu/s/0zC
